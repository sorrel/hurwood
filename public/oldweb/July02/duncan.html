Duncan's Page
Updated 11/07/2002

Duncan works as a software engineer in the daytime. The rest of the time he tries to entertain his daughter, wife and son.

He can never decide which is his favourite season, and so is enjoying the current English Summer which thinks it's an Autumn.

Below is a summary of his current thinking:

Books
I'm currently reading Fast Food Nation, by Eric Schlosser. Other than that I'm looking forward to the new Tom Stoppard plays, which come out in August.

Football
The World Cup was fun, but better at the beginning than the end. I was happy to see France, Italy and Portugal fail so badly, and happy to see Korea and the USA do so well. I wasn't that surprised with England's performance, but it was ok. My other team, Brighton and Hove Albion are spending the Summer without a manager, which must be saving them a few quid.

Games
I've been selling all my old consoles and games (against the wishes of Ebay, who seem to want to keep closing my auctions). Instead we've been playing German Board Games like Carcassone and The Settlers of Catan. It makes a very nice change, although I would like to play a few more computer or console games when the new systems have settled down a bit. Of the three consoles out at the moment I'd prefer to have a go at the GameCube.
