Shelley, the Pursuit
Updated 06/09/2001


I read the above book rather too much in the 1990s, and wrote a poem about it. Trying to stuff references to the book in every line doesn't improve it. Can you spot them all?


to settle my account
the fear of pursuit kept us awake
with delight at the thought
at what we had done; their
arms full of bundles and eyes open wide
as we get to the harbour
and for the first time
can see that my promise
is all they require

(the tracks we left behind
were notes that I must credit
with providing revolution
and shelter from this weather)
 the crossing was rough, it kept us awake
as we huddle together
on the top deck; the passengers laugh
with the crew at their jokes
and look over us slowly
as if we don't know
the way that their thinking
increases the cold

(forming a community
was all we entertained
like a party of assassins
we illuminate the way)
 the landing was easy
and together, arm in arm
we walk across the sandbank
and saw the work begin
from a carriage to the building
while reports of other storms
charged tyranny with passion
and put scars upon the wall

(I will face them without flinching
and set fire to all my boats, will
draw electricity from fingers
and will never let you go, I will
be honest with my feelings
dear sister of my soul
for it's better to burn constantly
than not to burn at all)
 I will contain these violent storms
and will wrap them up in cloth
that wraps around your skin
and pulls us both along;
I will negotiate a loan
so these words will not be lost
that will fill our sails with wind
and leave our bodies strong

and will give it to the world
to do the things I promise
