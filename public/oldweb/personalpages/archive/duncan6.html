Duncan's Page

Page last updated on the 21st of May 2005

This is Duncan's page, which he fills with his idle thoughts.

This time, possibly just to annoy my children, I've written them some poems:

Summer

A pond-skater, skating
On the pond, and in the stream
If I could just be anything
I think that's what I'd be.

I'd wander on the water
Where the current goes,
Letting all the ripples
Wash between my toes.

I'd smile at other skaters
And wave at all I meet,
I would watch the fishes darting
Underneath my feet.

I would tip-toe on the water
When danger's close at hand,
And wonder just hard it was
To walk upon the land.

And then, when no-one's watching
I'd skate a pirouette,
I'd finish with a flourish
And never miss a step.

I'd make a massive circle
With ever-growing rings
As dragonflies patrol the skies
With iridescent wings.

A pond-skater, skating!
That's the thing to be
If I could walk on water
On summer days, like these.

France

The thinker thinks
The waitress waits
The people wear fashionable macks;

Some of them sketch
Some of them shrug
None of them ever eat snacks.

The girl on the bridge met a breathless boy
Who worried what everything meant;

With strong natural passion
She gave him a croissant
Which he ate while looking intense.

Wild Animals

If anyone suggests that you keep
Your eyes on their pets for a day,
Before you agree check their pedigree
And see if they're wild or they're tame.

Ask them to tidy your room
Or lay the table for dinner;
If, with roaring and noise they eat your soft toys,
You probably should reconsider.

For the snarling beyond the locked door
There might be a straightforward reason;
To be on the safe side put your brother inside
And see if he ends up eaten.

Norway!

Norway the cold
Norway the tall
Norway for mountains and trees!

Norway the wet
Norway the dark
Norway for jumpers and skis!

Norway for beer!
Norway for fish!
Norway for Midsummer Sun!

Norway for deer!
Norway for boats!
Norway for culture and fun!

If you should make your way
To Norway, there's no way
You wouldn't love what they've done!

Pets

If ever the world gets you down
Your pet will still be your best friend;
They're joyful and wild
And with big doleful eyes
Their love never comes to an end.

Your pets will not hide how they feel
Or lock up their hearts in a box;
They're loyal and gentle
Polite and respectful:
They're everything humans are not.
