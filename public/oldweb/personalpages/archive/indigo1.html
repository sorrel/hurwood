Indigo's Page

Page last updated on the 9th of July 2003

This page is Indigo's space. She has very kindly allowed us an interview, which is reproduced below.

Mummy painted Indigo's curtains

Daddy: Hi Indigo, how are you feeling today?

Indigo: Alright.

Daddy: How old are you, Indigo?

Indigo: I'm three.

Daddy: You've chosen an octopus for your animal on this page. Why's that?

Indigo: I like octopus.

Daddy: What is it about an octopus that you particularly like?

Indigo: I like their legs.

Daddy: What do you like best about our house, Indigo?

Indigo: I like Bambi. He's a soft toy. A deer.

Daddy: Do you want to learn to read?

Indigo: Yes. I'll get a book. You can read it. You can tell me the words.

Indigo brings Daddy a book about baby animals, and they read together.

Daddy: Do you like making up your own stories?

Indigo: Yes. Chicken, chicken, where do you go? Rabbit rabbit where do you hop? Pig, pig, where do you snort?.

Daddy: What would you like to do when you're older - when you grow up?

Indigo: A football scorer. I want to kick footballs.

Indigo demonstrates kicking a football around the room.

Daddy: What musical instruments do you like playing?

Indigo: Cymbals. I can play a tune on them. They're very loud. Can you help me get them out the cupboard Daddy?

Indigo gets the cymbals, and plays a tune with Daddy on tambourine.

Daddy: What music that Mummy and Daddy play do you like?

Indigo Sings: Light is for the world.

The song is "This light is for the world" by The Waterboys.

Daddy: You like dressing up, don't you? What do you like dressing up as?

What Indigo has to face each day

Indigo: A cowboy.

Daddy: What's good about being a cowboy?

Indigo: I like fishing.

Daddy: Some people believe you have a problem with food, Indigo. So, what's your favourite meal?

Indigo: Peas. And fish fingers. And waffles.

Daddy: Is there any food you don't like so much?

Indigo: Lettuce.

Daddy: What's your favourite place to visit?

Indigo: Grandma and Grandpas.

Daddy: Is there a message you would like to send to the world?

Indigo: My house. My alphabet book. Can we play in a band again?

Daddy and Indigo play another tune and the interview is concluded.
