Indigo's World of Art

Page last updated on the 24th of January 2005

Indigo has a great love of art, and for this update she's sharing some of her favourite pictures, and showing us a few of her own.

"Snow White"

Snow White

Indigo has been enjoying Fairy Tales recently, and has drawn Snow White for us.

Asked what she likes about Snow White, Indigo says she likes "her face, and the person."

In addition, Indigo would like to add"Snow White's crown, and her hair bobbles."

She would also like everyone to notice that Snow White is drawn "next to her magic cupboard."

Snow White is drawn using felt-tip on ordinary A4.

 

 

 

 

 

Biff, in collage

Biff

Biff is a girl from the children's program The Magic Key. However, should you watch the program it is possible that you wouldn't recognise Biff from her protrait here.

Indigo says she is most pleased with "the hearts, and the green stuff, and the black dots."

Asked why she decided to make collage portrait Indigo asserts "I liked drawing, and sticking and making stuff."

 

 

 

 

 

Cloud Watching

Cloud Watching

This is the first of the pictures in her room that Indigo has chosen.

This one is called Cloud Watching, and is by Anita Jeram.

Asked to identify the elements that encouraged her to further the appreciation of the image, Indigo explained "I like their ears, and I like the grass."

Midnight Run

Midnight Run

This is Indigo's second picture from her room.

This second rabbit themed picture is called Midnight Run, and is by Nazran Govinder.

In her comments about this picture, Indigo said: "I like the colour, and the red spots. I like the tail, and I like the rabbit because it looks so big."

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Indigo, in her room

Indigo, in her room

Indigo wanted a picture of her sea-creature themed room.

To make it more artistic, we gave her a choice of a few filters over the image. This is the one she chose.

In particular, she would like to draw your attention to "the starfish, and the fish" to her right.

Those wishing to know the somewhat laborious details of the effect are directed towards Photoshop Elements, and the magic wand tool combined with the glowing edges filter.
