Indigo's Book Review

Page last updated on the 14th of August 2003

Indigo has decided to share a few of her favourite books with you. While she can't yet read, she very much enjoys books being read to her.

Her choice of books, however, has been mostly determined by the pictures found within them.

Indigo's favourite book

Toffee finds sleep a problem

Toffee's Night Noises by Sally Chambers

Indigo enjoys the adventures of Toffee, the cat who shares her interests in having naps, making new friends with small animals, and staying up all night being worried by unusual noises.

During her favourite part of the book Indigo likes hearing about when "Toffee climbs the tree after he hears the banging crashing whallop! noise".

Indigo also enjoys Toffee takes a nap, and sympathises with Toffee's inability to find peace in his noisey garden. She likes the drawings in both books, particularly the bees in Toffee takes a nap. Indigo also has the remaining books in the sequence: Toffee in Trouble, and Toffee's New Friend. She wishes there were more books about Toffee.

Indigo enjoys the art of this book

The Wolf Howls

My Many Coloured Days by Dr. Seuss

This contains a sweet little poem, but one that doesn't talk down to children. When coupled with the full-page illustrations it makes a very dramatic depiction of changing moods. It was first published in the 1990s, with the pictures created to accompany the text twenty years after the lines were written.

Particularly effective are the cool green fish, and the buzzy bees. And of course: The Wolf! The Wolf contains Indigo's favourite lines:

Then come my black days.
Mad.
And Loud.
I howl.
I growl at every cloud

Indigo says about it "I like the horsey in it! And the wolf, I like the wolf!". As usual, Indigo likes the things that also slightly frighten her.

Indigo likes poems very much, and enjoys making her own rhyming words, particularly words that rhyme with "Namister", her favourite made up non-word.

A typical ladybird book

Lambkin eats some dinner

Many Different Ladybird Books

Indigo likes the tersely versed Ladybird books mostly written by A.J. McGregor. Mostly published between 1940 and 1960 these books now show their age in the subject matter they address. However, we don't have a problem with 1950s morals every once in a while.

Their advantage (apart from the price - they can be found in most second half bookshops for under 50p each) lies in the length of story, and strictly rhyming verse. The rhythm of the words lends itself very well to reading aloud, and makes the experience more entertaining for reader as well as listener (at least, we hope it does).

Indigo's favourite character is Lambin, shown here tucking into her dinner during Bunny's First Birthday. As the books says:

Bunnies at their lettuce salad
Crunched and munched with shinning eyes;
Golden buttercups for Lambkin
Made her stare in glad surprise.

The Rabbits are surprised by snow

Little Fern's First Winter by Jane Simmons

For quite some time this was Indigo's favourite book. It features the adventures of Fern and Bracken as they experience their first snowstorm.

As with many children's books, this arctic encounter results in one of them getting lost, and Fern has to dig through the snow to find Bracken. All ends safely as they curl down safely back in their burrow with their Mummy.

When asked why she likes this book so much Indigo says "It's got Fern and Bracken in it!", and wishes there were more books about the adventurous rabbits.

A Polar Animal, yesterday

Polar Animals by Paul Hess

Indigo also enjoys factual books, with Polar Animals being one of her early favourites. On the globe the first place she points to is the North Pole saying "Polar Bears", then pointing to the South Pole saying "Penguins".

Indigo wanted the Walrus picture here "Because it's got Tusks!" (and probably because it frightens her a little), but likes all the Polar Animals, especially the Sea Lions and Seals.

Indigo also likes books about Farms, and Topsy and Tim books about daily chores. Topsy and Tim books are good as they tend to constantly emphasise correct behaviour at all times!

A Bush-Baby at night

Small Animals, a picture book

Indigo has a few photography books, but is always drawn back to Small Animals, though not for exactly the best reasons. She is fascinated by this picture of the Bush-Baby at Night, and will look at it to such an extent that we had to remove the book from her, and hide it.

While we hoped this book would lead to a love for baby rabbits and other lagomorphs and rodents, it has instead led to a greater interest (and fear of) eyes staring out of the dark. Indigo said "I like the dark, and the eyes, I like the eyes." when asked about why she liked this book so much.

Hopefully when Indigo next encounters the book she will find other pictures of small animals of interest. We don't hold out much hope for that soon...
