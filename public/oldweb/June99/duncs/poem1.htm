The Marxists Have a Party

They waited in the corridor
and together read the signs
to mean that history's wheel
was falling on it's side;

They opened up the chamber
and moved the tables from
their pre-Marxist positions
to where they all belonged;

They laid out glasses on them,
first taking off the cloth
and disposing of material
they knew would never wash;

They struggled in the classroom
and brought the boxes in,
they collected works around them
and then dispensed the drinks;

They put food upon the tables
and cheeses by the wine,
made the engines of their party
almost run on time;

They split apart collectively
and decided as a group
they should start the revolution,
and began to eat the food;

They talked throughout the evening
and agreed they thought the same
level of dissension
was acceptable in name;

By midnight it was over
and the guilty stains were on
the files they left behind them,
but everyone had gone.
